Writing Samples

I wish I could say I always loved writing, but that would certainly be a fabrication. Throughout my adolescence I genuinely disliked it, preferring writing’s more popular cousin: talking. Why? Talking is easy. Talking is fun. Talking doesn't enable you to re-write the same sentence until your brain can no longer derive meaning from words. In summary, writing is a lot of work.

I eventually changed my tune. I realized that activities—like writing—that require work can actually be really fun (or, at the least, rewarding). In my early 20s, I resumed reading for pleasure, which allowed me to find a love for the written word. While I am not what I would consider a “writer” with a capital W, I do consider myself a thoughtful and experienced writer in the practical sense.

In my professional life, writing consumes roughly half my day, from project plans to emails to everything in between. In UX research and design—and most professions I suppose—understanding how to communicate to a diverse audience effectively through writing is crucial to the success of any project.
